Caring Crochet

Caring crochet items doesn’t have to be difficult with these tips. You’ve accidentally spilled your latte down the front of your favourite crochet crop, or maybe you’ve discovered that your favourite crochet dress is overrun with pills. What do you do now?

These tips on caring for crochet items will help you keep your beloved handmade creations in good shape for years to come. These tips will help you take care of the crochet items you already have. Don’t fret about spending tons of time and money on a beautiful design only to ruin it in the wash or wear.

1. Read labels to know how to wash your projects

Crochet products do not require everyday wash. How do you wash crochet items, anyway? Plopping that gorgeous delicate crop top in your next load of laundry could have disastrous results, with everything from shrinkage to the item losing its shape. To care for your crochet items hand-wash or use the delicate cycle of your washing machine when laundering your crochet items. In addition, invest in a sweater rack so you can dry your handmade items on a flat surface. You want to treat those items as carefully as possible!

2. Spot clean a small stain on larger or more delicate items

While it’s annoying to discover a stain or spill on a beloved handmade item, not all hope is lost. Start by blotting up any excess crumbs or liquids on the stained crochet item. Make it easier to treat stains by keeping basic laundry care items on hand. There are plenty of additional ways to keep your crochet works in progress and completed projects organized.

3. Do not use scissors to cut extra threads

If you are a layman and aren’t aware about crochet technicalities , do not I repeat do not try to cut extra threads or yarn fizz with the help of a scissors. If the yarn from any part of the project gets cut, the entire product is left as a useless piece of fabric which can no longer be repaired. A hanging yarn could be bared rather than a wasted product.

4. Before you store any crochet product, make sure it is dry

Before bidding any crochet product in the wardrobe make sure it is clean and completely dry the project. And I mean completely dry it. Any moisture left in the fibers will cause problems.
